Many individuals with disabilities employ various tools and products in order to help them in everyday life. Here is a selection of these products as well as brief descriptions.

Mobility Assistance

Wheelchair

Wheelchairs assist a multitude of people and serve a variety of functions, whether it be long term solutions or short term assistance, but one thing is common: mobility is independence.

Mobility Transfer

Difficulty getting up from chairs is a common issue among those with less mobility than before, and also contributes to a feeling of limited freedom. Mobility Transfer Systems aid senior citizens as well as others who have a hard time getting up.

Mobility Transfer

Dining Tools

Gyro Spoon

Gyroscope spoons help stabilize eating utensils for people with unsteady hands, including those suffering from Parkinsons Disease. With the Gyro Spoon, the task of eating can be less difficult and or messy. Gyro Spoon

Easy Open Tool

This tool is designed to help with people with arthritis, as it makes it easier to open bottles and jars. Additionally, this tool can aid those with less control over gripping and squeezing motions. Easy Open Tool

Daily Life

Universal Assistive Device

In order to help people achieve a stronger grip in a variety of everyday tasks, such as washing the dishes or raking leaves.

Universal Assistive Device

Head Pointer

For people without the ability to use their arms or hands, the Head Pointer can allow creative people to draw with only their heads. Head Pointer

Products for the Blind

Screen Reader

One of the most universal tools for using the internet, Screen Readers convert text to speech for the blind or hard of sight. It is integral for access to technology for those without sight. Screen Reader

Braille Keyboard

It is integral for accessibility of technology for those without sight. The Braille Keyboard allows the blind to type without seeing the keys. A Braille Keyboard helps bridge the gap between analog methods and technology. Braille Keyboard

Technology for the Disabled

Hands Free Mouse

The Hands Free Mouse allows the user to control a mouse with movement of their head. This allows people without the conventional tools for using the internet to access technology. Hands Free Mouse

Eyegaze Pointer

For those without the ability to move their head or arms, the Eyegaze Pointer tracks the movement of the eye as a mouse. This way, even those with greater disabilities are able to access the internet with ease. Eyegaze Pointer
